#### **1\. Mission: The Wake-Up Call (QS. Al-Alaq: 1\)**

* **Mission Card:**  
  **MISSION: DECODING THE FIRST COMMAND**

*اِقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِيْ خَلَقَ*   
"Read with the name of your Lord who created." (QS. Al-Alaq: 1\)

Why was the very first command from Allah "Read" (Iqra), instead of "Worship"? Uncover the logic behind this. In your opinion, what is the most important thing a person should "read" or study in this world before doing anything else?

#### **2\. Mission: The Human Potential (QS. Al-Alaq: 2\)**

* **Mission Card:**

**MISSION: DECODING THE ORIGIN**

*خَلَقَ الْاِنْسَانَ مِنْ عَلَقٍ*   
"He created man from a clinging substance." (QS. Al-Alaq: 2\)

This verse mentions humans were created from a "clot" (something humble/low). Yet, Allah intends to elevate human status. Based on your discussion, what is the "hidden potential" within humans that allows us to achieve greatness in this vast world?

* **Dossier Log:** (Header: *Mission: The Human Potential*)

#### **3\. Mission: The Generous Mentor (QS. Al-Alaq: 3\)**

* **Mission Card:**

**MISSION: DECODING THE RABB**

*اِقْرَأْ وَرَبُّكَ الْاَكْرَمُ*   
"Read, and your Lord is the Most Generous." (QS. Al-Alaq: 3\)

Allah introduces Himself as *Al-Akram* (The Most Generous). If He is truly that generous, how should we approach Him when we want to ask for something or learn from Him? Should we feel fear, or should we feel secure?

* **Dossier Log:** (Header: *Mission: The Generous Mentor*)

#### **4\. Mission: The Knowledge Tool (QS. Al-Alaq: 4-5)**

* **Mission Card:**

**MISSION: DECODING THE WEAPON**

*اَلَّذِيْ عَلَّمَ بِالْقَلَمِۙ عَلَّمَ الْاِنْسَانَ مَا لَمْ يَعْلَمْ*   
"Who taught by the pen. He taught man that which he knew not." (QS. Al-Alaq: 4-5 )

This verse mentions the "Pen" as the primary tool of learning. Why is the pen so special that Allah highlights it? What should we "write down" or leave behind in our lives so that our knowledge doesn't fade and can benefit others?
